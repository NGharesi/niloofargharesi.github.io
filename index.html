Human Cortical Dynamics of Embodied Decision-Making, Outcome Monitoring, and Reward Prediction Errors

Niloofar Gharesi

PhD Thesis · McGill University · 2026

This work investigates how the human brain integrates perception, action, and feedback during decision-making using magnetoencephalography (MEG). The thesis examines embodied decision-making, outcome monitoring, and reward prediction error-like signals, showing how cortical dynamics support adaptive behavior under uncertainty.

Published Work

Evaluation of Abstract Rule-Based Associations in the Human Premotor Cortex

Niloofar Gharesi, Lucie Luneau, John F. Kalaska, Sylvain Baillet

NeuroImage · 2023

Examined how the human premotor cortex encodes abstract rule-based associations during passive observation using MEG. The results show that premotor activity reflects both correct and incorrect observed outcomes, even in the absence of overt motor actions, supporting a role for sensorimotor circuits in embodied decision-making and interpretation of non-biological events.

Extended State Observer-Based Control of Heartbeat Described by Heterogeneous Coupled Oscillator Model

Niloofar Gharesi, Mohammad Mehdi Arefi, Alireza Khayatian, Zahra Bahrami

Proposed an observer-based control framework to study and regulate cardiac rhythm dynamics using a heterogeneous coupled oscillator model. This work combines nonlinear dynamics, synthetic ECG modeling, and control theory to analyze cardiac electrical behavior and suppress sinus tachycardia.

Investigated how the human brain evaluates decisions under uncertainty using MEG. This work shows that post-decision neural dynamics reflect internally generated outcome expectations together with feedback-driven evaluation signals, including reward prediction error-like responses.
