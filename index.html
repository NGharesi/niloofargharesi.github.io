Published Work

Neural Outcome Evaluation System (MEG)

Niloofar Gharesi · John F. Kalaska · Sylvain Baillet · 2026

1. Problem

Understanding how the brain evaluates decision outcomes under uncertainty, and whether reward prediction error-like signals emerge in deterministic settings.

2. What I Built

  • Built an end-to-end MEG pipeline including experimental design, REB approval, participant recruitment, and data acquisition
  • Engineered time-frequency features in alpha and beta bands across cortical regions
  • Developed models linking neural dynamics to decision uncertainty and behavioral outcomes

3. Outcome

Identified beta-band signals reflecting context-dependent outcome evaluation, showing RPE-like dynamics strongest when correct outcomes were least expected.

Neural Signatures of Post-Decision Outcome Expectation and Evaluation in Human Sensorimotor Choice Behavior

Niloofar Gharesi, John F. Kalaska, Sylvain Baillet

bioRxiv · 2026

Investigated how the human brain evaluates decisions under uncertainty using magnetoencephalography (MEG). This work reveals that post-decision neural dynamics reflect both internally generated outcome expectations and feedback-driven evaluation signals, including reward prediction error-like responses.

Evaluation of Abstract Rule-Based Associations in the Human Premotor Cortex

Niloofar Gharesi, Lucie Luneau, John F. Kalaska, Sylvain Baillet

PMC · 2023

Examined how the human premotor cortex encodes abstract rule-based associations during passive observation using magnetoencephalography (MEG). The results show that premotor activity reflects both correct and incorrect observed outcomes, even in the absence of overt motor actions, supporting a role for sensorimotor circuits in embodied decision-making and interpretation of non-biological events.
